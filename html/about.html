Artist's Statement

Throughout my life I can recall finding pleasure in observing things that are not traditionally considered to be beautiful - a dirty wall, an old chair, a stack of towels. By carefully observing and painting a subject of this nature, I can begin to uncover what it is about that particular thing that initially grabbed my attention. This period of careful observation has become a powerful experience for me, resulting in me feeling more in touch with the physical world.


Similar to my experience of sitting with and painting a subject, I want my paintings to feel quiet and contemplative. By installing large, minimal, abstract paintings adjacent to smaller, representational paintings I hope to create a tranquil environment for viewers as they look. The larger works appear to be abstractions of certain details found in the smaller, realistic paintings and help to connect the overriding themes of simplicity, the ordinary and being cognitively present in the physical world.
